Real estate activity

Rental activity, development or acquisition plans, depreciation, basis, 1031 exchange questions, and real estate tax accounting concerns.

Estate and trust considerations

Trusts, estate planning documents, gifting, 1041 estate or trust returns, 709 gift tax questions, and coordination with attorneys or other advisors.

Return and document readiness

Prior returns, entity records, financial statements, K-1s, income sources, multi-state issues, and upcoming decisions that should be reviewed early.

The Risk

Complex financial situations can create planning gaps when they are reviewed too late.

Many high-value taxpayers only revisit their structure during tax filing season. By then, some decisions may already be locked in, documentation may be incomplete, and planning windows may be narrower.

Entity and ownership structure LLCs, S corporations, partnerships, trusts, and related entities need careful coordination.
Real estate tax complexity Depreciation, basis, passive activity rules, and transaction timing can affect planning options.
Multi-state or high-income exposure Income sources, residency, and investment activity can create added filing and advisory considerations.
Missed documentation Planning discussions are more useful when records, entity details, and prior returns are reviewed early.
